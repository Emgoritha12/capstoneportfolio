Case Study: Marvel Fandom

Case Study: Marvel Fandom My major examines digital and interactive media with a focus on online communities, participatory culture, and social communication. Through a case study of the Marvel fandom, I analyzed how fandoms function as digital communities by using social capital, grassroots creativity, crowdsourcing, and interactive media platforms to engage millions of users. I also explored the limitations of these systems, including the rise of toxicity and online bullying in large-scale communities, demonstrating a critical understanding of both the benefits and challenges of digital media ecosystems.

Revision: Conformity and Dimensions of Credibility as Persuasion

My major focuses on communication, social influence, and persuasion, with an emphasis on how individual behavior is shaped within group and cultural contexts. Through research on conformity, credibility, and social influence, I examined concepts such as normative and informational influence, social proof, social loafing, and group dynamics, as well as key dimensions of credibility including expertise, goodwill, trustworthiness, and composure. This work highlights my academic focus on analyzing human behavior, persuasion, and interaction within social systems.

Mental Health In Greek Life: A Multimodial Data-Driven Story

This project explores the relationship between participation in Greek life and mental health among university students through an anonymous, multi-institutional survey of fraternity and sorority members. The study examines self-reported mental health before and after joining Greek organizations, as well as perceptions of peer pressure and mental health stigma within these communities.
